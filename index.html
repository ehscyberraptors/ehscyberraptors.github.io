Cyber Raptors
Eaglecrest High School Cybersecurity Club
Helping students understand how digital systems work and how to protect them. No experience required. Any grade can join.

About the Club

We are a club at Eaglecrest High School focused on learning cybersecurity concepts and artificial intelligence development. Our goal is to understand how the internet and security tools function internally and to recognize when and how they should be used for defense.

The organization does not support unauthorized access or misuse of technology. Instead, it promotes responsible security practices and awareness within the digital world.

The club is open to students with no prior experience who are interested in learning. It also provides a competitive environment for students who already have technical background and wish to improve their skills while collaborating with others at the school.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before joining.

Question: When are your meetings?►
Answer: Mostly Tuesdays and Fridays, days may change as our sponsor (Mr. Seely) has a tight schedule.
Question: What do we do during those meetings?►
Answer: We learn a new thing every two weeks. Week one is the learning portion and week two is when we do TryHackMe or HackTheBox rooms together.
Question: What is TryHackMe and HackTheBox?►
Answer: They are platforms that provide cybersecurity training environments with hands-on lab rooms designed to simulate vulnerable systems.
Question: How much time do we have to give up?►
Answer: Not much. We only expect members to attend meetings. Outside of that, nothing extra is required unless you are helping manage the club.
Question: Do I need to have my own computer?►
Answer: No. We work in the AP CS classroom which already has computers. On lab days we work together as a group.
Question: Is this competitive or collaborative?►
Answer: The environment can be competitive for those with prior experience, but for beginners it is primarily a learning opportunity. Those who are new can build foundational skills while more experienced members refine their abilities.
Question: Can beginners join or is it too late?►
Answer: It is never too late to begin learning. Many of our members join with little or no prior experience, and the club provides a supportive environment to build a strong foundation in cybersecurity.
Question: Is this club recognized by the Eaglecrest activities office?►
Answer: Yes, we are registered as a student-led club with the activities office.
Question: Who runs the club and how many members do you have?►
Answer: Several students contribute to running the club. As of now we have over 10 active members.
